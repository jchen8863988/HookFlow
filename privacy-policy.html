This Privacy Policy explains how HookFlow (“we”, “us”) handles information when you use the HookFlow mobile application (“App”). It is intended to meet Apple App Review transparency expectations and common privacy-law disclosure practices (including GDPR-style notices where applicable). If you do not agree, do not use the App.

1) Scope

This policy covers the HookFlow App for iOS/iPadOS obtained through the Apple App Store. Apple’s collection of information in connection with the App Store (for example account and transaction data) is described in Apple’s Privacy Policy. Where this policy conflicts with mandatory Apple terms for App Store apps, Apple’s requirements apply.

2) Summary

  • HookFlow helps you plan and produce short-form marketing-style video content (templates, previews, export).
  • On-device storage: drafts, jobs, optional analytics events (if enabled), consent choices, UI preferences, language choice, and backend URL/token you save are stored locally on your device unless you use features that send data elsewhere.
  • Optional “cloud” features: if you configure a backend URL and enable related options, the App may send requests to that server (operated by you or a third party you choose). That server may call AI providers (e.g. OpenAI-compatible APIs, Google Gemini) or fetch public data sources when you enable them.
  • In-app consent: before full use, the App asks whether you allow optional analytics and optional personalization (used to gate certain cloud/AI-related flows). You can change effective choices later where the App provides controls.
  • Not “tracking” for ads: HookFlow does not use your information for cross-app advertising tracking as defined by Apple’s rules, and does not sell your personal information to data brokers. Optional analytics in the current client design are stored on-device and are not transmitted to us by default.

3) Permissions and on-device access

The App may request access only as needed for features you use:

  • Photo Library (read): to let you pick photos or videos you choose for previews and marketing-style exports. The App accesses only items you select via the system picker (no broad library scanning for unrelated purposes).
  • Photo Library (add / save): to save exported videos to your library when you choose to save or share in a way that writes to Photos.

System permission prompts show Apple’s standard descriptions derived from the App’s usage strings. If you deny access, related features may be unavailable.

4) Information we process

4.1 You provide or generate in the App

  • Creative inputs: text such as app name, audience, hook ideas, template selections, and similar content you enter.
  • Media you select: images/videos you choose from your library for workflows that use them.
  • Backend configuration: base URL and optional authentication token stored locally for connecting to your configured server.
  • Public-insights configuration: keywords, optional App Store app identifiers, and toggles for enabled public sources — stored locally and sent to your configured backend when you use those features.

4.2 Generated or stored automatically on your device

  • Job and draft identifiers and history needed to show job status (stored locally).
  • Optional analytics events (only if you opt in): events such as screen interactions the App records for product improvement are stored on-device in the current implementation (for example, so you can review them in debugging/diagnostic UI). They are not transmitted to us (the developer) by default.

4.3 Processed via your configured backend or third-party APIs

When you use cloud generation, competitor/public-insights jobs, uploads, or optional server-side metrics ingestion, payloads travel to the backend URL you configured. That environment may further send data to:

  • AI providers (e.g. OpenAI, Google Gemini) for script/generation tasks, depending on server configuration;
  • Public platforms and feeds (e.g. Reddit, Hacker News, App Store RSS, X/Twitter) for public content when such integrations are enabled on the server;
  • Optional video engagement metrics: if implemented in your build, aggregated events (e.g. play/complete) may be sent to your configured backend when analytics-style consent allows — subject to your server implementation.

Those third parties are independent of the App developer and have their own privacy policies. We do not control their practices.

5) How we use information

  • Provide core App functionality (templates, editing, preview, export queue).
  • Honor your consent choices (essential vs optional analytics vs personalization/cloud paths).
  • Maintain reliability and security of the App.
  • Comply with law and respond to lawful requests where applicable.

6) Legal bases (EEA, UK, and similar regimes)

Where GDPR-style laws apply, we rely on:

  • Performance of a contract / necessity — to deliver features you request;
  • Consent — for optional analytics and optional personalization/cloud-related processing where presented;
  • Legitimate interests — for security, fraud/abuse prevention, and service integrity, balanced against your rights.

7) Sharing

  • We (the developer) do not sell your personal information. We do not operate an advertising network or broker data from the App for sale.
  • Your configured backend operator receives what you send to that URL — which may include prompts, uploads, and job payloads.
  • Apple receives standard App Store / platform telemetry under Apple’s policies when you use Apple devices and services.
  • AI and data platforms process information only as routed by your backend configuration when you use those features.

8) Sale / sharing under U.S. state privacy laws

Some U.S. states grant residents rights regarding “sale” or certain “sharing” of personal information. HookFlow does not sell personal information for monetary consideration. If we ever engage in analytics or partnerships that constitute “sharing” under applicable law, we will update this policy and App Store disclosures.

9) Retention

  • On-device data remains until you delete the App, clear app data (where the OS allows), or remove items using in-app controls.
  • Server-side retention depends entirely on whoever operates your configured backend and their logs/backups.

10) Security

We apply reasonable safeguards appropriate to a consumer creativity app. No method of storage or transmission is completely secure. Protect your device and any backend tokens you configure.

11) Your rights and choices

  • Managing choices in the App: open Settings → Privacy & data (or equivalent) to review optional analytics and personalization, and use Reset privacy & data choices there if you want to clear your saved consent and choose again on next launch. Language and other preferences are available under Settings as well.
  • Consent: adjust optional analytics/personalization where the App provides controls.
  • Access / deletion (local data): uninstalling the App or clearing app data often removes locally stored information on many devices.
  • EEA/UK and similar rights: you may have rights to access, rectify, erase, restrict, object, or port data — contact us at the email below. We may need to verify requests as permitted by law.
  • California / other U.S. state rights: you may have additional rights (access, deletion, correction, opt-out of certain processing). Contact us to exercise applicable rights.

13) International transfers

14) App Tracking Transparency (ATT)

HookFlow does not use cross-app tracking for advertising as a core requirement of the App. If a future version introduces “tracking” as defined under Apple’s rules, we will obtain permission through Apple’s App Tracking Transparency framework where required and update this policy before enabling such behavior.
