This Privacy Policy describes how HookFlow (“we”, “us”) collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the HookFlow mobile application (“App”). It is designed to align with Apple App Review expectations and common privacy laws (including GDPR-style transparency for EU users). If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1) Summary

  • HookFlow helps you ideate and structure short-form marketing content. Some features can call a backend server URL you configure and optional AI providers (e.g., OpenAI and/or Google Gemini) when you enable cloud features.
  • By default, much of your content (drafts, consent choices, local job history) is stored on-device. Analytics and personalization are optional and gated behind in-app consent.
  • If you enable “public insights” features, the App may send configuration (keywords, optional App Store app id, source toggles) to your configured backend to fetch public signals from third-party sources you select.

2) Information we collect

We collect information in the following categories, depending on how you use the App:

2.1 Information you provide

  • Creative inputs: text you enter (e.g., app name, audience, pain points, template selections).
  • Backend configuration: the backend base URL and optional authentication token you save in the App (stored locally on your device).
  • Public insights configuration: keywords, optional App Store app id, and toggles for public data sources (stored locally and may be transmitted to your configured backend when enabled).
  • Photos / screen recordings: if you choose to import media from your photo library for preview/export, the App accesses only the items you select through the system picker.

2.2 Information collected automatically

  • Diagnostics and usage (optional): if you opt in to analytics in the App’s privacy choices, we may collect limited usage events to understand feature usage. If you do not opt in, we do not collect analytics events in the default configuration described here.
  • Crash data: if you use Apple’s crash reporting or third-party crash tools in the future, that collection must be disclosed separately in App Store Connect and here. (If not enabled, none is collected by HookFlow itself.)

2.3 Information from third parties

When you use cloud features, your configured backend may retrieve or process information from third-party services you enable, such as:

  • Google Gemini / OpenAI-compatible APIs (when configured on the server)
  • Public web sources (e.g., Reddit, Hacker News, App Store RSS, X) when enabled

Those services are independent controllers/processors with their own policies. We do not control their data practices.

3) How we use information

We use information to:

  • Provide core App functionality (templates, previews, exports, job tracking)
  • Operate optional cloud features you request (AI generation, competitor-style analysis)
  • Improve the product when you opt in to analytics/personalization
  • Comply with law and enforce our terms

4) Legal bases (EEA/UK users)

Where GDPR applies, we rely on:

  • Contract / necessity: to provide the App features you request
  • Consent: for optional analytics/personalization and certain optional data processing you enable
  • Legitimate interests: to secure the App, prevent abuse, and improve reliability (balanced against your rights)

5) Sharing and processors

We may share information with:

  • Your configured backend: anything you send to your server URL is under your (or your operator’s) control.
  • AI providers: prompts and related job payloads may be processed by providers such as Google Gemini and/or OpenAI when your server is configured to call them.
  • Public data sources: when enabled, your backend may fetch public posts/pages/feeds according to your configuration.
  • Apple: standard App Store distribution and platform services are subject to Apple’s privacy policy.

6) Data retention

  • On-device data remains until you delete the App, clear app data (where supported), or use in-app controls (e.g., clearing local job history).
  • Server-side retention (if you operate a backend) is determined by your server configuration and hosting provider.

7) Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures appropriate to the nature of the App. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

8) Your rights and choices

  • Withdraw consent: use the App’s privacy choices screen to disable optional processing where available.
  • Access / deletion: for purely on-device data, uninstalling the App or clearing app data is the most complete deletion path on many devices.
  • EEA/UK rights: you may have rights to access, rectify, erase, restrict, object, and port data. Contact us at [CONTACT_EMAIL].

9) Children’s privacy

HookFlow is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). Do not use the App if you are not old enough to consent where required.

10) International transfers

If you use cloud features, your information may be processed in countries where your AI provider and/or hosting services operate. When required, appropriate safeguards (such as standard contractual clauses) may be used by your chosen providers.

11) App Tracking Transparency (ATT)

HookFlow does not require cross-app tracking for core functionality as described in this policy. If a future version introduces tracking (as defined by Apple), we will request permission through Apple’s App Tracking Transparency framework where required and update this policy accordingly.
